Facebook Ads Campaign

I ran a Facebook ad campaign and there were two main target markets that I identified for this campaign:

  • Employees in Related Industries who are looking to transition into the UX industry
  • Students in College/Grad School in cities with top Human-Computer Interaction Programs who are starting to look for their first job.

    Below are the exact audience demographics entered into Facebook:

    An A/B test was run to compare which of the two audiences would have a higher click-through rate and engagement with the same ad.

    The ultimate goal of the campaign was to drive views and ebook downloads on the below landing page, created using Unbounce.

    The CPC campaign ran for 5 days and had a total budget of $100. It was a little disappointing to see that the campaign had a relatively low CTR of 0.55% with a total of 62 unique clicks out of 11,255 impressions. However, it was encouraging to see that the CPC was $1.54, which is below the average CPC of $1.86 across all industries.

    Using the Facebook Pixel, I was track that 5 people actually clicked on the green "Get Your Ebook" button on the landing page, which is about a 8% conversion rate among the 62 people who ended up clicking on the Facebook ad.

    Regarding the A/B test, both audiences had almost the same number of clicks, although employees had both a higher CTR and a higher CPC due to 1000 fewer impressions and 2 fewer clicks. Students had higher engagement with the ad (7 reactions and 2 post saves), likely because they are more active on social media to begin with.

    If I had the chance to run the campaign again, I would further narrow down the target audience to improve the CTR (such as changing the age range for employees from 18-35 to 22-35 since most employees are 22+) and also tailor specific ads to each of the two audiences.

    Email Marketing Campaign

    I created a drip email campaign and the goal of campaign was to convert customers to become a student of Springboard’s UX Design course.

    Email 1

    Your free UX Design Careers ebook has arrived

    The purpose of the email is to get people to view the UX Design Careers ebook, so that they have a free resource from Springboard and are more inclined to read further emails.

    Email 2

    What a student loved (and hated) about our UX Design Course

    The purpose of the email is to convince readers to view the blog post so that they find out more about the UX Design course and are willing to sign up in the final email.

    Email 3

    Final chance to join the May Cohort for our UX Design Course - just a few spots remaining!

    The purpose of the email is to convert people to sign up and become a UX Design course student.

    Capstone Project

    For my capstone project, I am currently participating in the Google Ad Grants Online Marketing Challenge. This gives me the opportunity to create and execute online Google Ad campaigns for a real nonprofit using a $10,000 USD monthly budget.

    I was paired with The Plato Society, an educational non-profit dedicated to providing intellectually-curious adults with the opportunity to continue learning and form a community with like-minded individuals. The Plato Society offers their members who pay an annual fee the chance to participate in 3 semesters worth of stimulating study discussion groups focused on a wide variety of topics. The target market are adults over 50 years old and seniors, including the retired or semi-retired, who reside in Los Angeles and can attend these study discussion groups in person.

    The overall goals of the marketing campaign are to:

    • -increase awareness and web traffic
    • -attract younger members (ie. anyone 50+ that may not be a retiree yet)
    • -encourage people to either sign up to become a member or at least sit in on a course to decide if the Plato Society is right for them.

    I started by running 3 main campaigns:

    Plato Society Branded Campaign
    - includes Plato Society-related keywords. This ensures that we control the brand message and we own the real estate when someone searches "Plato Society".
    Retired/Elderly/Senior Campaign
    - includes keywords related to retirees, elderly and seniors.
    Related Discussion Clubs Campaign
    - includes keywords of various clubs such as book club, socrates club or debate club that have a similar target market to the Plato Society.
