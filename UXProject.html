Sebastian

In February 2019, I completed Springboard's UX Design course. As part of the curriculum, I had to apply the concepts and skills that I learned by completing a capstone project that would take me through the entire UX design process from start to finish. The end result was Sebastian, a mobile app designed from scratch to help shoppers better navigate grocery stores.

The Problem

Grocery stores are large and can be hard to navigate, with aisles upon aisles of products to choose from and occasional store layout changes. Some people don't enjoy grocery shopping and would like to get it over with as quickly as possible, while others are looking for ways to save money on their groceries.
