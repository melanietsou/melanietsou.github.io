REBNY

As part of the curriculum for my Springboard Digital Marketing course, I ran a Facebook ad campaign to market one of Springboard’s free resources, an ebook titled “A Beginner’s Guide To Careers in UX Design”.

There were two main target markets that I identified for this campaign:

  • Employees in Related Industries who are looking to transition into the UX industry
  • Students in College/Grad School in cities with top Human-Computer Interaction Programs who are starting to look for their first job.

Below are the exact audience demographics entered into Facebook:

An A/B test was run to compare which of the two audiences would have a higher click-through rate and engagement with the same ad.

The ultimate goal of the campaign was to drive views and ebook downloads on the below landing page, created using Unbounce.

The CPC campaign ran for 5 days and had a total budget of $100. It was a little disappointing to see that the campaign had a relatively low CTR of 0.55% with a total of 62 unique clicks out of 11,255 impressions. However, it was encouraging to see that the CPC was $1.54, which is below the average CPC of $1.86 across all industries.

Using the Facebook Pixel, I tracked that 5 people actually clicked on the green "Get Your Ebook" button on the landing page, which is about a 8% conversion rate among the 62 total people who ended up clicking on the Facebook ad.

Regarding the A/B test, both audiences had almost the same number of clicks, although employees had both a higher CTR and a higher CPC due to 1000 fewer impressions and 2 fewer clicks. Students had higher engagement with the ad (7 reactions and 2 post saves), likely because they are more active on social media to begin with.

If I had the chance to run the campaign again, I would further narrow down the target audience to improve the CTR (such as changing the age range for employees from 18-35 to 22-35 since most employees are 22+) and also tailor specific ads to each of the two audiences.
